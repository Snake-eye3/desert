About

I'm a Sudanese professional with a background that crosses borders — literally and figuratively. From translating in conflict zones to building data pipelines for humanitarian programmes, I've always been drawn to work that sits at the intersection of rigour and meaning.

My toolkit spans MEAL frameworks, SQL & Python data analysis, humanitarian field coordination, and Arabic–English translation. I studied political science and have spent years making sense of complex systems — whether that's a conflict map, a dataset, or a policy document.

Outside of work, I read widely, write on topics I care about, and believe that thinking clearly is the most underrated skill in any field.
